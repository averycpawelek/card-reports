How to read this

Under the current five criteria (gem ≥45% + spread ≥3× + 8+ raw copies + $250–750 + 2018-19 or newer): zero buys. The gem bar and the spread bar veto each other — every card with a big spread gems low (10s being scarce is why the spread is big), and the card that gems high (Wemby, 81%) has a compressed spread because 10s are everywhere.

Under a profit gate instead (expected profit ≥ $200 AND ROI ≥ 50%): SGA Silver #184 is a buy (+$382 expected, 86% ROI, backed by 32 PSA-10 sales and 17 live raw copies in the buy band), with Edwards #258 the next one to watch. The full argument is in the criteria tuning brief.

Caveats

Gem-rate selection bias: PSA’s published gem rate reflects cards submitters pre-screened before paying grading fees. Buying raw off eBay sight-unseen should be underwritten at a haircut — SGA at half its published 16% still clears roughly +$160, and that’s the honest range to discuss.

This is a partial sweep (~6% of the in-band pool, 4 product lines, basketball only). The next full sweep runs with deeper comps and wider coverage; expect more candidates like SGA, not fewer.
