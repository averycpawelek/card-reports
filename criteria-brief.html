What we did

We ran the full pipeline three times (15 → 30 → 100 players) with all five criteria enforced, then rebuilt the scan so eBay itself only serves listings priced inside the $250–750 buy band with graded slabs excluded (criterion 4 applied server-side). That final sweep valued the 100 most promising in-band card models with real subqueries: live raw asking prices, PSA-10 sold comps, and — for survivors — the PSA gem-rate panel.

Result: exactly one card in ~8,700 in-band models cleared spread + band + liquidity… and it fails the gem bar. Zero buys.

*Expected profit = gem_rate × (PSA-10 price − raw) − $20 grading, computed with each card's real PSA population data (conservative: PSA-9 outcome valued at $0). These are the two best cards we have ever validated end-to-end — each fails exactly one criterion, and a different one.

Why this happens: gem rate and spread fight each other by construction. A high gem rate means PSA 10s are common → the PSA-10 price compresses toward raw → spread shrinks below 3×. A big spread means PSA 10s are scarce and premium → the gem rate is low. Demanding both ≥45% and ≥3× selects a market corner that barely exists: across every in-band card with two-sided data, the best spreads were 2.8×, 2.8×, 2.6× — and the 3×+ card gems at 16%.

Recommendation

Keep as hard gates: raw $250–750 (capital per card), ≥8 raw copies (entry liquidity), season 2018-19+ (modern focus). These are operational constraints and they filter cheaply and correctly.

Replace the gem bar + spread bar with the profit math they're standing in for. The pipeline already computes expected profit using each card's actual gem rate and actual prices. A gate of expected profit ≥ $200 AND ROI ≥ 50% passes both exhibits above (+$382 / 86% ROI and +$676 / 91% ROI) and still rejects every low-gem low-spread dud — the math punishes a 16% gem rate exactly in proportion to what it costs.

If you want a floor anyway: gem ≥ 25% or spread ≥ 4× (either extreme can carry the EV) plus the profit gate. Today that yields the same two buys.

Caveats

Comp depth: the exhibits above are re-verified with ~6 months of sold history behind each figure (SGA: 32 PSA-10 sales, 17 live raw copies). The sweep-wide funnel counts on this page were measured with an earlier, shallower comp pass and will be re-measured on the next full sweep — expect the per-gate counts to shift, not the shape of the argument.

Coverage: 100 valuation slots per run against ~8,700 in-band models. Slots are cheap now (~2 unlocker credits each); if the tuned criteria pass more cards, we raise the cap, not the player list.
