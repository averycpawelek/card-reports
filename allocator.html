Show the math behind this result

1 · Each copy is a two-outcome bet with outcome-dependent PSA fees

win (prob p): profit = PSA10_price × (1 − 13.25% fee) − raw − fee(PSA10 value tier)
miss (prob 1 − p): profit = r × raw × (1 − fee) − raw − submission_fee
stake = raw_median + submission_fee    

The upcharge mechanic: you submit at the cheap tier, and PSA bills a card that grades into a higher value bracket at that bracket's fee — so the win leg of a $3,000 card carries a ~$299 fee while its miss leg carries only the submission fee. p is the PSA population gem rate with small-sample shrinkage: (pop10 + 20 × 0.40) / (pop_total + 20).

2 · Per-copy expectation, spread, and clock

μ = p × win + (1 − p) × miss    σ² = p × (win − μ)² + (1 − p) × (miss − μ)²
cycle = (turnaround + logistics) ÷ 30.44 + sale_queue  sale_queue = live PSA-10 supply ÷ monthly sales, measured per card

5 · The outcome statistics

4,000 portfolios simulated: every copy independently gems with probability p. Reported: mean, share below $0, 10th/90th percentiles, plus the capital-weighted average cycle and the annualized rate if proceeds redeploy. NOT simulated: market prices moving during the lockup (the per-player cap is the guard), your own buying pressure on thin supply, and PSA turnaround variance — the days you enter are point estimates.
