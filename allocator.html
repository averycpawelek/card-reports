Show the math behind this result

1 · Each copy is a two-outcome bet with outcome-dependent PSA fees

win (prob p): profit = PSA10_price × (1 − 13.25% fee) − raw − fee(PSA10 value tier)
miss (prob 1 − p): profit = r × raw × (1 − fee) − raw − submission_fee
stake = raw_median + submission_fee    

The upcharge mechanic: you submit at the cheap tier, and PSA bills a card that grades into a higher value bracket at that bracket's fee — so the win leg of a $3,000 card carries a ~$299 fee while its miss leg carries only the submission fee. p is the PSA population gem rate with small-sample shrinkage: (pop10 + 20 × 0.40) / (pop_total + 20).

2 · Per-copy expectation, spread, and clock

μ = p × win + (1 − p) × miss    σ² = p × (win − μ)² + (1 − p) × (miss − μ)²
cycle = (turnaround + logistics) ÷ 30.44 + sale_queue  sale_queue = live PSA-10 supply ÷ monthly sales, measured per card

3 · Ranking: risk-adjusted profit per dollar

Greedy fill in score order; copies added until supply, the Kelly cap, the player cap, or the bankroll binds. Score ≤ 0 at this risk setting = excluded.

3½ · The Kelly exposure cap (n*)

G(n) = Ep ~ Beta(α, β) Ek ~ Bin(n, trust × p) log(1 + (k × win + (n − k) × miss) ÷ bankroll)    n* = argmax G(n)

The pop panel's gem rate is an estimate, not the truth — and every copy of a card shares the same truth, so a batch is one correlated bet, not n independent ones. Each card carries a Beta posterior over its true rate (pop counts + the same pseudo-counts the shrinkage uses); n* is the batch size that maximizes expected log bankroll growth over that posterior. Deep pop → near-independent copies, n* is large. Thin pop → the "what if the true rate is on the low end" scenario hits every copy at once, and n* stops the buying early no matter how good the average EV looks.

5 · The outcome statistics

Each simulated portfolio first draws every card's true gem rate from its Beta posterior (thinned by gem trust), then grades that card's copies at the drawn rate — so the batch correlation behind the Kelly cap is in the simulated tails too, not assumed away. Reported: mean, share below $0, 10th/90th percentiles, plus the capital-weighted average cycle and the annualized rate if proceeds redeploy. NOT simulated: market prices moving during the lockup (the per-player cap is the guard), your own buying pressure on thin supply, and PSA turnaround variance — the days you enter are point estimates.
